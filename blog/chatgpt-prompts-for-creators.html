15 ChatGPT Prompts for Content Creators That Actually Work (2026)

By WeadFamily Projects · July 23, 2026 · 10 min read

Most ChatGPT prompt lists are garbage. They give you "Act as a content creator..." and call it a prompt. That's not a prompt — that's a wish.

Real prompts include: role, context, format constraints, tone, and a specific output structure. Here are 15 that actually produce usable content.

YouTube & Long-Form Video

1. The Hook Generator

You are a YouTube scriptwriter. Generate 10 opening hooks (first 3 seconds) for a video titled "[TITLE]". Each hook must: create curiosity or state a bold claim, be under 15 words, and make it impossible to scroll past. Format as a numbered list with the hook text in quotes.

2. The Script Structure Builder

Take this video topic: "[TOPIC]". Create a complete script outline with: (1) Hook - 3 seconds, (2) Intro - 10 seconds, (3) Main content broken into 3-5 sections with timestamps, (4) CTA - 15 seconds. For each section, include 1 bullet point of what to cover. Target length: [X] minutes.

3. The Thumbnail Concept Generator

You are a YouTube growth strategist. For a video titled "[TITLE]", generate 5 thumbnail concepts. Each concept should include: a visual description (what's in the frame), text overlay (max 4 words), emotion to convey, and why it would get a high CTR. Reference current YouTube thumbnail trends.

TikTok & Short-Form

4. The Viral TikTok Hook

Generate 15 TikTok hooks for a video about [TOPIC]. Hooks should be under 10 words and fall into these categories: curiosity gap, bold statement, relatable POV, contrarian take, and how-to. Format: [Category] "Hook text" — why it works.

5. The Trend Adaptation Prompt

Take this trending TikTok format: "[TREND DESCRIPTION]". Adapt it for the niche of [YOUR NICHE]. Give me 5 variations with: full script (15-30 seconds each), suggested audio type, and which hashtags to use. The adaptations should feel native to the niche, not forced.

6. The Caption + Hashtag Optimizer

Write a TikTok caption for a video about [TOPIC]. Requirements: first line must be a hook (not repeating the video), total caption under 150 characters, include a question to drive comments, and 5-7 relevant hashtags mixing broad (#fyp) and niche-specific. Explain why each hashtag was chosen.

Instagram & Social

7. The Carousel Outline

Create a 7-slide Instagram carousel about [TOPIC]. For each slide, give me: slide number, headline (max 6 words), body text (max 30 words), and visual suggestion. The carousel should tell a complete story from problem to solution. Last slide should be a CTA to [YOUR CTA].

8. The Reel Script

Write a 15-second Instagram Reel script about [TOPIC]. Include: on-screen text (word by word), voiceover script, suggested trending audio type, and 3 text overlay variations for the cover. The Reel should be saveable/savable content (educational or relatable).

Blog & SEO

9. The SEO Blog Outline

Create a blog post outline targeting the keyword "[KEYWORD]". Include: suggested H1 title (under 60 chars for SEO), meta description (under 155 chars), 5-7 H2 sections, 2-3 H3 sub-sections under each H2, and a FAQ section with 3 questions. Search intent: [INFORMATIONAL/COMMERCIAL/TRANSACTIONAL].

10. The Meta Description Writer

Write 5 meta description variations for a blog post titled "[TITLE]" targeting the keyword "[KEYWORD]". Each must be: under 155 characters, include the target keyword naturally, end with an action verb, and avoid clickbait. Pick the best one and explain why.

Email & Newsletter

11. The Subject Line Split Test

Write 5 email subject lines for a newsletter about [TOPIC]. Mix these styles: curiosity gap, number list, bold statement, question, and personal/story. Each under 50 characters. Predict the open rate ranking from best to worst and explain why.

12. The Welcome Sequence

Write a 3-email welcome sequence for new subscribers interested in [NICHE]. Email 1: Welcome + set expectations (150 words). Email 2: Deliver quick win + soft pitch (200 words). Email 3: Story + direct CTA to [PRODUCT] (250 words). Include subject lines and preview text for each.

Monetization

13. The Product Idea Generator

Based on my content niche ([NICHE]) and audience (describe: [AUDIENCE]), suggest 5 digital product ideas. For each: product name, format (ebook/template/course/toolkit), price point, why my audience would buy it, and the top 3 features it must include. Rank by ease of creation vs revenue potential.

14. The Sales Page Copywriter

Write sales page copy for a digital product called "[PRODUCT NAME]" priced at $[PRICE]. Structure: headline, sub-headline, problem agitation, 3 benefits (not features), social proof placeholder, what's included, FAQ (3 questions), guarantee, and CTA. Tone: confident but not hypey. Target audience: [AUDIENCE].

15. The Affiliate Review Template

Write a product review outline for [PRODUCT NAME] in the [NICHE] space. Include: quick verdict (3 sentences), who it's for, who it's NOT for, 3 pros, 2 cons, pricing breakdown, alternative comparison, and final recommendation. Tone: honest, specific, no fluff. Include a disclosure statement.
