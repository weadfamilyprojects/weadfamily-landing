100 ChatGPT Prompts That Actually Work for Content Creators

By WeadFamily Projects · July 23, 2026

If you search for "ChatGPT prompts for creators" on Google or social media, you’ll find hundreds of lists containing "500 prompts to make you a millionaire."

But when you actually copy and paste those prompts into ChatGPT, you quickly realize something: most of them are garbage.

They are usually generic, vague, and single-step prompts like "Write a video script about productivity." The result is almost always a dry, boring draft filled with clichés and robotic language. It’s useless.

The truth is, AI is only as good as the context and structure you give it. If you feed it a generic prompt, you get generic content. But if you feed it structured, specific, and constrained prompts, it can write copy that sounds indistinguishable from a seasoned human creator.

To save you months of trial and error, I have compiled a teaser list of 10 highly effective, battle-tested ChatGPT prompts that actually work. They are organized by creator category, and they are ready for you to copy, paste, and customize.

Category 1: Video & Scriptwriting (YouTube & Short-Form)

1. The Hook Generator

```text

"You are a viral YouTube scriptwriter. I am making a video about [TOPIC] for [TARGET AUDIENCE]. Give me 5 distinct hook options (under 10 seconds each) to stop viewers from scrolling. Include: 1) A pattern interrupt, 2) A curiosity-gap question, 3) A contrarian statement that goes against common wisdom, 4) A shocking statistic, and 5) A micro-story. Explain why each works psychologically."

```

2. The Retention-Optimized Outline

```text

"Create a detailed outline for a [DURATION]-minute video on [TOPIC]. Structure the outline using the 'Problem-Agitation-Solution' copywriting framework. For each section, specify the estimated time, the main talking points, suggested B-roll visuals, and a transition hook to keep the viewer watching the next section."

```

3. The YouTube Title & Thumbnail Concept

```text

"Based on this video concept: '[TOPIC]', generate 10 click-worthy YouTube titles under 55 characters (so they don't get cut off on mobile). Avoid clickbait that lies; instead, use curiosity gaps and emotional triggers. For the top 3 title choices, describe a matching thumbnail design concept (composition, text overlay, and psychological focus)."

```

Category 2: Social Media (LinkedIn, Instagram & Twitter/X)

4. The LinkedIn Thought-Leadership Post

```text

"Translate this rough idea into a conversational LinkedIn post: '[INSERT IDEA/LESSON]'. Requirements: Start with an attention-grabbing first line (hook), use short paragraphs of 1-2 sentences max, use bullet points for readability, include one personal mistake or struggle to build authenticity, and end with an engaging open-ended question to drive comments. Avoid corporate buzzwords."

```

5. The High-Value Twitter/X Thread

```text

"Convert this article/concept: '[PASTE CONCEPT/TEXT]' into an educational Twitter/X thread of 6-8 tweets. Tweet 1 must be a powerful hook that promises a specific outcome. Each subsequent tweet must share exactly ONE actionable takeaway or example. Tweet 10 (the final tweet) should be a call to action to [YOUR CTA]. Use line breaks and emojis sparingly for clean formatting."

```

6. The Instagram Carousel Outline

```text

"Create an outline for a 7-slide Instagram carousel post about [TOPIC]. For each slide, write: 1) The visual description (what to show), 2) The exact text overlay (maximum 15 words), and 3) A brief caption note. Slide 1 must be a scroll-stopping hook; Slide 7 must be a clear call to action."

```

Category 3: Blog & SEO Copywriting

7. The Topic Cluster & Keyword Plan

```text

"You are an SEO strategist. My core topic is [CORE TOPIC]. Generate a topic cluster plan containing 8 blog post titles that would rank on search engines. For each title, provide: a suggested target primary keyword, three secondary keywords, and the search intent (informational, commercial, navigational)."

```

8. The Section-by-Section Draft Writer

```text

"You are an expert blogger. We are writing a blog post based on this outline: [PASTE OUTLINE]. Write ONLY the section titled '[SECTION NAME]'. Requirements: Use a conversational, smart-friend tone, include specific examples, use short paragraphs, and smoothly integrate the keyword '[KEYWORD]' naturally twice. Do not write the introduction or conclusion; focus entirely on this section."

```

Category 4: Email & Newsletter Marketing

9. The High-Open Subject Line Generator

```text

"I have written an email about [EMAIL TOPIC]. Write 10 subject lines and matching preview texts. Give me: 3 curiosity-based subject lines, 3 benefit-driven subject lines, 2 urgency-based subject lines, and 2 contrarian subject lines. Keep all subject lines under 40 characters for mobile display optimization."

```

10. The 3-Part Welcome Email Sequence Outline

```text

"Create an outline for a 3-part automated email welcome sequence for subscribers who download my freebie about [TOPIC]. Outline: Email 1 (Deliver the freebie + introduce myself), Email 2 (Share my single biggest mistake/lesson), and Email 3 (A soft pitch for my paid product, [PRODUCT NAME]). For each email, provide the objective, subject line ideas, and the primary call to action."

```
