🎯 Content Creator Command Center: The Ultimate Notion Setup Guide

Your all-in-one system for managing content across TikTok, Instagram, YouTube, and beyond. No more scattered notes, missed deadlines, or forgotten brand deal follow-ups.

📋 What You'll Build

10 interconnected Notion databases that run your entire content operation

Step 1: Create Your Dashboard Page

  1. Open Notion and create a new page called "Content Creator Command Center"
  2. Add a cover image and icon that match your brand
  3. At the top, add a Callout block with a welcome message:
"Welcome to your Command Center. This is where content gets planned, created, and tracked. Let's build something great today."
  1. Below the callout, create a 2-column layout:
    • Left column: Quick links to all sections
    • Right column: Today's priority tasks

Step 2: Build the Content Calendar Database

This is the heart of your Command Center.

  1. Create a new database called "Content Calendar"
  2. Set the view to Calendar layout

  • Kanban Board (grouped by Status) — for your content pipeline
  • Table View — for bulk editing
  • Board by Platform — to see what's queued per platform

Step 4: Build the Script Vault

Where your scripts live.

Property NameTypeDescription
TitleTitleScript name
PlatformSelectTikTok, Instagram Reel, YouTube, etc.
HookTextThe opening line (most important!)
Full ScriptTextComplete script
B-Roll NotesTextWhat to film/capture
Call to ActionTextWhat you want viewers to do
StatusStatusDraft, Review, Approved, Recorded
Linked ContentRelationLinks to Content Calendar

Script Template structure:

  • Hook (first 3 seconds)
  • Intro (who you are, what this is about)
  • Body (main content/value)
  • CTA (call to action)
  • B-Roll Shot List (bullet points of what to film)
  • Caption Ideas (2-3 caption variations)
  • Hashtag Bank (relevant hashtags)

Step 9: Connect Everything Together

  1. In Content Calendar, add a Relation to Script Vault (one-to-one)
  2. In Content Calendar, add a Relation to Brand Deals (many-to-one)
  3. In Idea Inbox, add a Relation to Content Calendar
  4. In Content Calendar, add a Rollup from Performance Tracker
  5. In Brand Deals, add a Rollup from Content Calendar

Step 10: Design Your Dashboard

Add these linked database views to your main page:

  • Today's Tasks — Content Calendar filtered by Status: "Scheduled" or "Filming"
  • This Week's Posts — Content Calendar filtered by Publish Date: This Week
  • Hot Ideas — Idea Inbox filtered by Priority: 🔥 Hot
  • Active Brand Deals — Brand Deals filtered by Status: not "Paid"
  • Monthly Goals — Goals filtered by Status: "In Progress"

Pro Tips

  • Use templates inside databases: Create page templates for each content type so you don't start from scratch
  • Color-code your platforms: Give TikTok pink, Instagram purple, YouTube red for instant visual recognition
  • Set a weekly review: Every Sunday, review Performance Tracker and plan the upcoming week
  • Batch your scripting: Write 5 scripts at a time, then batch-film them
  • Track everything: Even failed content teaches you something — log it all
  • Use Notion's mobile app: Capture ideas on the go directly into your Idea Inbox
