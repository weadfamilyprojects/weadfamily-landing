The 7-Day Launch Roadmap

Follow these steps in order. Each day builds on the previous one. By Day 7, you'll have a fully launched channel with 10 scheduled videos.

Day 1: Pick Your Niche

Choose ONE niche from the proven list below. Don't overthink this — pick the one that interests you most.

Proven Faceless Niches (with sub-niches):

  • 🏠 Home & Design — room makeovers, organization hacks, DIY projects, interior tips
  • 🤖 Tech & AI — AI tool reviews, tech news, productivity apps, coding tips
  • 💡 Motivation — success quotes, mindset tips, discipline content, stoicism
  • 💰 Finance — budgeting, side hustles, investing basics, money myths
  • 📦 Organization & Productivity — life hacks, systems, routines, habit building

Action: Pick your niche. Write it down. Don't move to Day 2 until you've chosen.

Day 2: Set Up Your Accounts

Create accounts on all three platforms. Use the same channel name across all of them for brand consistency.

Platform Setup Checklist:

  • YouTube Shorts: Create a new Google account → Set up YouTube channel → Upload profile picture (use AI-generated or Canva) → Write channel description → Enable Shorts
  • TikTok: Create account → Switch to Business account (free) → Add bio link → Set up profile
  • Instagram Reels: Create account → Switch to Creator account → Add bio → Link to other platforms

Naming tips: Keep it short, memorable, and relevant to your niche. Avoid numbers. Examples: "AIHabitLab", "FacelessFinance", "DesignHacksDaily"

Action: Set up all three accounts today. Upload a profile picture. Write a 1-sentence bio for each.

Day 3: Set Up Your AI Tool Stack

Sign up for the tools below. All have free tiers — you don't need to pay anything to start.

Essential Tools (Free Tier):

  • ChatGPT (chat.openai.com) — Write your scripts. Free tier is sufficient.
  • ElevenLabs (elevenlabs.io) — AI voiceovers. Free tier = 10,000 characters/month (~10 short scripts).
  • CapCut (capcut.com) — Video editing. Completely free, browser-based or desktop app.
  • Pexels (pexels.com) — Free stock footage. Unlimited downloads, no attribution required.
  • Pixabay (pixabay.com) — Backup stock footage and images. Also free.

Optional (Paid, upgrade later):

  • InVideo AI ($20/mo) — Auto-generates videos from text prompts. Worth it once you're earning.
  • Murf.ai ($19/mo) — More voice options if ElevenLabs free tier isn't enough.
  • Pictory ($19/mo) — Another auto-video option with stock footage matching.

Action: Create accounts on ChatGPT, ElevenLabs, CapCut, and Pexels. Bookmark them all.

Day 4: Write Your First 5 Scripts

Use the 10 Script Formulas in the next section. Pick 5 formulas and write one script for each using the fill-in-the-blank templates.

Each script should be 150-250 words (30-60 seconds when spoken). Shorter is better for Shorts/TikTok.

Process:

  1. Open ChatGPT
  2. Paste the formula template
  3. Fill in your niche details
  4. Ask ChatGPT to refine and optimize for retention
  5. Copy the final script

Action: Write 5 scripts today. Save them in a Google Doc or Notion page.

Day 5: Generate Voiceovers & Find Footage

Turn your scripts into voiceovers and find matching footage.

Voiceover Process:

  1. Open ElevenLabs
  2. Pick a voice that matches your niche (professional for finance, energetic for motivation, calm for home design)
  3. Paste your script into the text box
  4. Generate and download the audio file
  5. Repeat for all 5 scripts

Footage Process:

  1. Go to Pexels.com/videos
  2. Search keywords related to your script topic (e.g., "modern kitchen" for a home design video)
  3. Download 3-5 clips per video (15-30 seconds each)
  4. Organize by script number in folders

Action: Generate all 5 voiceovers and download footage for all 5 scripts.

Day 6: Edit & Schedule First 10 Videos

Edit your 5 videos in CapCut, then write 5 more scripts and repeat the process.

CapCut Editing Process:

  1. Open CapCut → New Project
  2. Import your voiceover audio first
  3. Add stock footage clips on top of the audio
  4. Cut footage to match the pacing of the voiceover
  5. Add text captions (auto-captions feature in CapCut)
  6. Add background music (CapCut has free music, keep volume low at 10-15%)
  7. Add a text hook in the first 3 seconds (big, bold text)
  8. Export in 9:16 aspect ratio (vertical)
  9. Export at 1080p quality

Pro tips:

  • Change visuals every 2-3 seconds to maintain attention
  • Add zoom effects on key moments
  • Use CapCut's auto-captions (85% of Shorts viewers watch with sound off)
  • Keep videos between 30-58 seconds (optimal for algorithm push)

Action: Edit 5 videos today. Write 5 more scripts and repeat the voiceover + footage process for a total of 10 videos.

Day 7: Launch & Optimize

Upload your 10 videos across all three platforms. Don't upload all at once — space them out.

Upload Schedule:

  • YouTube Shorts: Upload 2 per day, 8 hours apart (e.g., 9am and 5pm)
  • TikTok: Upload 2 per day, different times than YouTube
  • Instagram Reels: Upload 1 per day

Optimization Checklist (for each video):

  • Title: Use a curiosity-driven hook (e.g., "You're organizing your kitchen wrong" beats "Kitchen organization tips")
  • Description: 2-3 sentences with keywords. Include a call to action.
  • Tags/Hashtags: 5-10 relevant hashtags. Mix broad (#homedesign) and specific (#scandinavianminimalism)
  • Thumbnail: YouTube Shorts allows custom thumbnails — use a bold text overlay on a relevant image

First 30 Days Strategy:

  • Upload consistently (1-2 videos per day per platform)
  • Don't worry about views in the first 2 weeks — the algorithm is learning
  • After 2 weeks, check analytics: which videos performed best? Make more like those.
  • Engage with comments on every video (boosts algorithm signal)

Action: Upload your first batch today. Schedule the rest. You're officially a faceless creator. 🎉

10 Copy-Paste Script Formulas

Each formula includes the structure, a fill-in-the-blank template, and a real example. Just plug in your niche and topic.

Formula 1: The "Top 5 List"

Structure: Hook → List 5 items with brief explanations → CTA

Template:
"5 [topic] tips that actually work.
Number 5: [tip] — [one sentence explanation].
Number 4: [tip] — [one sentence explanation].
Number 3: [tip] — [one sentence explanation].
Number 2: [tip] — [one sentence explanation].
And number 1: [tip] — [one sentence explanation]. This one changed everything for me.
Follow for more [niche] tips."
Example (Home Design):
"5 home organization hacks that actually work.
Number 5: Use vertical space — wall-mounted shelves give you 3x more storage without buying bigger furniture.
Number 4: The 90-second rule — if it takes less than 90 seconds to put something away, do it immediately. Game changer.
Number 3: Drawer dividers — stop the junk drawer chaos. A $5 divider set transforms your kitchen.
Number 2: Label everything — not for organization, for accountability. When things are labeled, everyone puts them back.
And number 1: The one-in-one-out rule — for every new item you bring home, remove one. This one changed everything for me.
Follow for more home organization tips."

Monetization Playbook

5 proven ways to make money from a faceless channel. Start with #1 and #2, add the others as you grow.

1. YouTube Partner Program (Ad Revenue)

Requirements: 1,000 subscribers + 4,000 watch hours (or 10M Shorts views in 90 days)

Timeline: 2-4 months with consistent daily uploads

Earnings: $2-10 per 1,000 views (varies by niche). Finance and tech pay the highest RPMs ($5-15). Home/design averages $3-7.

How to qualify faster: Post 1-2 Shorts daily (Shorts views count toward the 10M threshold). Long-form videos (5-10 min) build watch hours faster. Do both simultaneously.

Pro tip: Enable monetization settings BEFORE you qualify. When you hit the threshold, earnings start immediately instead of waiting for review.

2. Affiliate Marketing

Requirements: None — start from Day 1

Earnings: $10-500 per sale, depending on the product

Best programs by niche:

  • Home & Design: Amazon Associates (home products), Wayfair affiliate, IKEA affiliate (via Awin)
  • Tech & AI: Amazon Associates (tech), AppSumo affiliate (software), tool-specific programs (ElevenLabs, Notion, etc.)
  • Finance: Credit card affiliates, investing apps (Robinhood, Webull), budgeting tools (YNAB)
  • Productivity: Notion affiliate, tool reviews with affiliate links
  • Motivation: Book links (Amazon Associates), course platforms (Skillshare, MasterClass)

How to promote: Mention the tool/product naturally in your video. Put the affiliate link in your bio and video description. Don't make it the main focus — make the content valuable, and the affiliate click is a bonus.

3. Digital Products

Requirements: An audience (even small — 500+ engaged followers is enough)

Earnings: $15-50 per sale, 100% margin

What to sell:

  • Template packs (Notion templates, prompt packs, script templates)
  • Mini-courses (video or text-based, teaching your specific system)
  • Cheat sheets and checklists (quick reference guides for your niche)
  • Full guides and playbooks (like this kit — comprehensive resources)

Where to sell: Gumroad (best for digital downloads), Etsy (for templates and planners), your own site

How to promote: Create a product that solves the #1 problem your viewers have. Mention it at the end of relevant videos. Link in bio. The key: your videos build trust, your products capture value.

4. Sponsorships

Requirements: 5,000+ subscribers with consistent engagement

Earnings: $50-2,000 per sponsored video (scales with audience size)

How to pitch:

  1. Make a list of brands that align with your niche
  2. Find their PR or marketing contact (check their website, LinkedIn, or email hello@brand.com)
  3. Send a short pitch: who you are, your audience size, engagement rate, and a content idea featuring their product
  4. Include your media kit (even a simple 1-page PDF with your stats and demographics)
  5. Follow up once after 7 days if no response

Sponsorship email template:

"Hi [Brand] team,

I run a [niche] channel with [subscriber count] subscribers and [average views] views per video. My audience is primarily [demographic] interested in [topics].

I'd love to create a sponsored video featuring [product]. I have an idea for a [content angle] that would showcase it naturally.

My rates: $[rate] for a 30-60 second integration.

Happy to share my media kit and recent engagement stats. Let me know if this is a fit!

[Your name/channel]"

5. Channel Flipping (Build & Sell)

Requirements: A monetized channel with consistent views

Earnings: $500-10,000+ per channel (based on monthly revenue)

What it is: Build a faceless channel to a profitable state, then sell it to someone who wants a head start. Platforms like Flippa, Fameswap, and Social Tradia facilitate these sales.

Valuation: Channels typically sell for 20-40x monthly revenue. A channel earning $200/month could sell for $4,000-8,000.

How to do it:

  1. Build a channel in a profitable niche (finance, tech, productivity)
  2. Grow to 1,000-5,000 subscribers with consistent views
  3. Enable monetization and earn for 2-3 months to establish a track record
  4. List the channel on Flippa or Fameswap with verified analytics
  5. Transfer ownership and get paid

Pro tip: The system in this kit is designed to be repeatable. Build a channel, sell it, build another. This is the most scalable faceless income strategy.

Bonus: AI Persona Trainer

Your channel needs a consistent voice and personality. Here's how to create one using AI.

Step 1: Define Your Persona

Answer these questions about your channel's identity:

  • Tone: Professional? Casual? Energetic? Calm? Authoritative? Friendly?
  • Perspective: First person ("I discovered...") or second person ("You need to...")?
  • Pacing: Fast and punchy? Slow and thoughtful?
  • Signature phrases: What 2-3 phrases will you use consistently? (e.g., "Here's the thing...", "Let me show you...", "Follow for more...")

Step 2: Create a Persona Prompt

Use this template in ChatGPT to generate scripts in your consistent voice:

"You are a [niche] content creator. Your channel voice is [tone], [pacing], and uses [perspective] perspective. Your signature phrases are: '[phrase 1]', '[phrase 2]', '[phrase 3]'.

Write a 45-second script about [topic] using this formula: [paste one of the 10 formulas from this kit].

Requirements:
- 150-200 words
- Start with a hook in the first 3 seconds
- Include one surprising fact or contrarian take
- End with a call to action to follow
- Maintain the voice and tone described above"

Step 3: Pick Your AI Voice

In ElevenLabs, pick ONE voice and stick with it for at least your first 30 videos. Consistency builds recognition. Tips for choosing:

  • Home & Design: Warm, calm, slightly slower pace. Try "Charlotte" or "Matilda" on ElevenLabs.
  • Tech & AI: Clear, energetic, slightly faster pace. Try "Adam" or "Antoni."
  • Finance: Authoritative, professional, measured. Try "Daniel" or "Josh."
  • Motivation: Intense, confident, strong pacing. Try "Brian" or "Sam."
  • Productivity: Friendly, approachable, clear. Try "Rachel" or "Lily."

Step 4: Create a Visual Identity

Even without showing your face, your videos should look consistent:

  • Color palette: Pick 2-3 colors for text overlays (use the same ones in every video)
  • Font: Use the same font in all captions and text overlays (CapCut defaults are fine — just pick one and stick with it)
  • Intro pattern: Start every video the same way (e.g., bold text hook on screen for 3 seconds, then voiceover begins)
  • Outro pattern: End every video with the same CTA format

The Key Insight

Consistency beats quality in the first 90 days. A mediocre video posted daily will outperform a perfect video posted weekly. The algorithm rewards consistency. Your audience builds trust through familiarity. Get the reps in.
